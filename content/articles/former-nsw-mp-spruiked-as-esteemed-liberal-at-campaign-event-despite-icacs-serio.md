A former New South Wales MP found by the Independent Commission Against Corruption (Icac) to have engaged in serious corrupt conduct was celebrated at a Liberal Party campaign event, despite his history of allegations.

## What happened

John Sidoti, a former Liberal minister and state member for Drummoyne, attended a campaign launch event for Stephanie Di Pasqua, his successor as MP and former staffer. At the event, which was headlined by the state opposition leader Kellie Sloane, he was feted as one of the "esteemed luminaries of the Liberal party." The event took place last week as part of the re-election campaign for Di Pasqua.

## Why it matters now

Icac found Sidoti had engaged in serious corrupt conduct, and he left the Liberal Party in 2021 amid the investigation. His presence and praise at a high-profile Liberal event raise questions about the party's approach to former officials with corruption findings.

## Why it matters

The incident highlights a potential conflict between political loyalty and public accountability. It also brings attention to how political parties handle the reputations and roles of individuals with past misconduct allegations.
