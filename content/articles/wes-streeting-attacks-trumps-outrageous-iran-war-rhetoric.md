UK health secretary Wes Streeting condemned Donald Trump’s Iran rhetoric as "incendiary, provocative and outrageous" and called the failure of recent US-Iran peace talks disappointing.

## What happened

Wes Streeting criticized Trump’s comments on Iran as "outrageous" and said the recent peace talks between the US and Iran had failed to produce results. In an interview on Sky News' *Sunday Morning With Trevor Phillips*, he described the situation as disappointing and emphasized the importance of continuing diplomatic efforts.

"As ever in diplomacy, you’re failing until you succeed," Streeting said. "So while these talks may not have ended in success, that doesn’t mean there isn’t merit in continuing to try."

## Why it matters now

Streeting’s remarks reflect the UK’s concern over the potential for increased tensions in the Middle East. The health secretary affirmed the UK’s position that diplomatic efforts remain essential to achieving stability in the region.
