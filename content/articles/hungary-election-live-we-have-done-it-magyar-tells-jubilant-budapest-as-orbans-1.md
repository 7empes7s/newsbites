Hungary’s long-serving prime minister, Viktor Orbán, was ousted after 16 years in power, with opposition leader Peter Magyar declaring victory in a jubilant speech in Budapest.

## What happened

Peter Magyar, leader of the center-left Democratic Coalition, told supporters in Budapest, 'We have done it,' after early election results showed a clear lead over Orbán’s Fidesz party. Orbán, who has led Hungary since 2010, is the first sitting prime minister in the country’s modern history to lose an election.

## Why it matters now

Orbán’s rule has been marked by a shift toward illiberal policies and a close alignment with Russia. His exit could signal a return to more pro-European Union and democratic norms in Hungary, a key EU member state.

## Why it matters

The election marks a historic shift in Hungarian politics. Magyar’s win could influence the country’s foreign policy and domestic governance, with potential ripple effects across the European Union.
