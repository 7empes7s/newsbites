The UN Secretary-General urged the United States and Iran to extend their recent ceasefire and reaffirmed the importance of respecting international law, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz.

## What happened

In a statement released on April 14, 2026, the UN chief emphasized the need for both countries to continue de-escalating tensions and to ensure the restoration of international navigation rights in the strategically critical Strait of Hormuz, a key maritime route for global oil shipments.

## Why it matters now

The Strait of Hormuz has been a focal point of geopolitical tension for weeks, with recent hostilities disrupting normal shipping operations and raising concerns about energy security. The UN chief warned that international law is being "trampled" in the region and called for renewed diplomatic efforts to stabilize the situation.

## Why it matters

The call for continued ceasefire and respect for international law reflects the broader international community's interest in preventing further escalation between two of the world's most influential powers. The stability of the Strait of Hormuz is crucial for global trade and energy markets.
