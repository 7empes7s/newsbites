## The actual constraint

Most early AI products do not fail because the model is weak. They fail because
the workflow is noisy, expensive, or impossible to verify under pressure.

That makes cost discipline an editorial and product requirement, not just an
engineering one.

## What disciplined teams do differently

- They define a cheap default model before adding premium fallbacks.
- They separate operator control from batch content generation.
- They treat every autonomous step as something that must earn its way into the stack.

## Why this matters for NewsBites

NewsBites is being built around a simple rule: the first publishing loop must be
repeatable and manually reviewable before it becomes broader or more autonomous.

That means:

- one editorial agent first
- one public product first
- one approval flow first

## The practical takeaway

If an AI system cannot explain what it changed, show evidence, and stay within a
budget, it is not ready to scale.
