The U.S. Department of Homeland Security announced on April 12, 2026, that it is investigating a claim alleging Rep. Eric Swalwell, a California Democrat, hired a Brazilian national as a nanny without legal work authorization. The claim was filed by Joel Gilbert, a known conspiracy theorist with a history of distributing anti-Barack Obama material to voters in 2012.

## What happened

The investigation was disclosed by The Guardian World, citing a report that the U.S. representative is facing scrutiny over the alleged employment of the Brazilian nanny without proper work authorization. The claim is part of a broader wave of scrutiny against Swalwell, who has also been accused of sexual misconduct and assault by four women, as reported by the San Francisco Chronicle and CNN. The congressman has denied the allegations.

## Why it matters now

This development adds to a growing list of controversies surrounding Swalwell, who is currently running for governor of California and serving in Congress. While the claim has not been proven, the involvement of a federal agency like DHS signals a level of seriousness to the allegations. The claimant, Joel Gilbert, is a public figure known for his past controversial actions, which raises questions about the credibility of the filing.

## Why it matters

The investigation highlights how public figures are increasingly subject to legal and political scrutiny over immigration-related matters. As the story unfolds, the focus will remain on the evidence DHS uncovers and whether it supports the allegations. For now, the claim remains under review, and no charges have been filed against the congressman.
