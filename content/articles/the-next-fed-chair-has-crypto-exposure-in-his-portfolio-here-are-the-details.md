The next Federal Reserve chair, Kevin Warsh, has disclosed that he holds crypto-related assets, including stakes in DeFi protocols, Ethereum scaling networks, a Bitcoin Lightning startup, and prediction markets. Warsh has pledged to sell all these assets before assuming his role.

## What happened

Warsh's financial disclosures, made public via a CoinDesk report, reveal his holdings in multiple crypto-related ventures. These include DeFi protocols, an Ethereum Layer 2 scaling network, a Bitcoin Lightning Network startup, and participation in prediction markets. Warsh has confirmed that he intends to divest these assets ahead of his anticipated appointment to the Fed.

## Why it matters now

Warsh, a former Fed governor and current nominee for the role of chair, has made public commitments to address potential conflicts of interest. His disclosures follow increasing public and political scrutiny of the personal financial ties of potential regulators and policymakers in the crypto space.

## Why it matters

Warsh’s crypto exposure raises questions about regulatory impartiality and public trust in the Fed's decision-making. His commitment to sell these assets reflects an effort to preempt concerns about potential conflicts of interest.
