## The shift underneath the noise

When rates stay meaningfully positive for long enough, the market stops
rewarding vague future stories as easily as it did during ultra-cheap money.
Capital is no longer free, and the cost of being wrong is no longer absorbed by
the next round of fundraising.

That changes what gets attention. Slide decks that used to open with a total
addressable market chart now open with a cash flow chart. Investor calls that
used to end with a vision statement now end with a question about working
capital.

It is not a revolution. It is gravity returning.

## What investors are looking for

The screening filters that matter most have not changed in decades. They are
just being applied again:

- **Clearer balance sheets** — fewer off-balance vehicles, fewer "adjusted"
  metrics, fewer footnotes that explain away the headline number.
- **Cleaner cash conversion** — revenue that turns into cash on a predictable
  schedule, not revenue that turns into receivables and hope.
- **Fewer moving parts in the core business** — one or two engines, not seven
  experiments funded by one good quarter.

Companies that already operate this way are quietly outperforming. Companies
that have to pivot toward it are paying for the pivot in real time.

## Why it matters

This does not mean growth is dead. It means growth now has to survive a more
skeptical environment. The bar for "interesting" rose, and the bar for "fund
this" rose with it.

For operators, that usually translates into a simpler rule: if the business
model needs a heroic macro backdrop to work, the market will eventually price
that fragility in. Sometimes early. Sometimes all at once.

## What to watch next

Three signals tell you whether the discipline is sticking or just a passing
mood:

- **Premiums on free cash flow** versus premiums on revenue growth in
  comparable peers
- **Refinancing terms** for mid-cap companies with maturities coming due
- **Equity raises** that explicitly fund operations rather than expansion

If the first two stay tight and the third stays rare, the regime is real. If
they loosen the moment a single soft inflation print lands, treat it as
temporary.

## The practical takeaway

The cheapest edge available right now is not a clever trade. It is reading the
statements carefully and trusting what they say. That used to be table stakes.
For a while, it was optional. It is table stakes again.
