The UN General Assembly is convening to debate a Security Council veto by China and Russia on actions related to the Hormuz Strait crisis, as humanitarian conditions in Lebanon continue to deteriorate.

## What happened

The UN General Assembly is meeting to address a Security Council veto by China and Russia, blocking potential measures regarding the strategic Hormuz Strait crisis. At the same time, Lebanon remains in the grip of a severe humanitarian crisis, with ongoing challenges for civilians under a fragile US-Iran ceasefire.

## Why it matters now

The veto highlights the geopolitical tensions between major powers, with China and Russia aligning to block decisions on a critical global chokepoint. Meanwhile, Lebanon's humanitarian crisis reflects the broader regional instability and the challenges of sustaining peace amid ongoing conflicts.

## Why it matters

The UN meeting underscores the limitations of international diplomacy in resolving crises when key Security Council members act in unison to block action. For Lebanon, the humanitarian crisis illustrates the human cost of geopolitical stalemates and the urgent need for coordinated aid and political solutions.
