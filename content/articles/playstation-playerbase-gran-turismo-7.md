PlayStation is testing a new kind of fan program. Instead of offering a beta
slot, a collector's item, or a normal contest reward, Sony says one selected
fan will be scanned into *Gran Turismo 7* as part of a new initiative called
The Playerbase.

The program opened on April 7 and starts with Polyphony Digital's racing game,
with Sony saying more PlayStation Studios titles could follow later.

## What the program actually is

According to PlayStation, The Playerbase is a multi-region fan-selection
program for players who want to tell their PlayStation story and potentially
appear in a PlayStation Studios game.

The first opportunity is tied to *Gran Turismo 7*. Sony says the selected
winner will appear in the game for a limited time and will also help create a
custom Fantasy Logo and a one-of-a-kind vehicle livery that will stay in the
Showcase menu permanently.

That means the winner is not just being scanned for a novelty cameo. Their
design work becomes part of the game's longer-lived customization layer.

## How selection works

Sony says entrants must be at least 18 and have a PlayStation online ID in a
participating territory. The company also says the process has multiple rounds.

The first round looks at a written submission and account history, including
originality, connection to PlayStation fandom, and signs of real engagement
with both the PlayStation brand and Polyphony Digital. The second round moves
to interviews, where Sony says it will judge authenticity, entertainment value,
community fit, and whether the person can represent PlayStation well.

Sony expects the *Gran Turismo 7* winner to be chosen in summer 2026.

## Why Gran Turismo 7 is the right first test

If PlayStation wanted to start this idea somewhere, *Gran Turismo 7* makes more
sense than most of its other franchises.

Sony describes the game as a large international community built around racing,
collecting, tuning, livery design, photography, and online competition. That
is exactly the kind of environment where a fan portrait, custom logo, and
vehicle livery can feel visible rather than hidden away.

The result is part brand campaign, part community reward, and part live-service
content design.

## What this says about fan marketing

The bigger idea behind The Playerbase is not hard to read. Sony is turning one
fan into official platform content and using the selection process itself as a
story about what being a PlayStation fan looks like.

That is a more involved move than the usual social campaign. It also gives Sony
something many game companies want: user-generated material that is curated,
high-control, and easy to share across marketing, community, and in-game
channels at the same time.

Whether that becomes meaningful for players depends on how often Sony repeats
the program and which games join next. One winner in one game can still feel
like a stunt. A recurring program across multiple studios would be a different
category of fan strategy.

## Why it matters

The immediate headline is simple: PlayStation is going to put one real fan into
*Gran Turismo 7*. The more useful read is that Sony is experimenting with a new
way to merge fandom, platform identity, and official game content.

If The Playerbase expands beyond *Gran Turismo 7*, it could become a new
template for how major publishers reward community loyalty without relying only
on giveaways or influencer campaigns. If it stays a one-off, it will still have
shown how far platform marketing is willing to go to make fandom itself part of
the product.
