Researchers have proposed a new citation recommendation system that integrates human citation patterns with semantic information to improve both accuracy and efficiency in scientific communication.

## What happened

A team of researchers has introduced a novel system called Profiler, a lightweight module designed to capture human citation behavior without incurring high computational costs or introducing bias. The system also introduces a new evaluation protocol—Inductive evaluation—to better simulate real-world citation scenarios. Alongside this, they developed DAVINCI, a reranking model that combines Profiler's insights with semantic data for improved performance.

## Why it matters

Citation recommendation systems are critical for validating and contextualizing scientific contributions. Current systems often fail to account for the nuances of human citation behavior and are tested under unrealistic conditions. The new approach aims to address these limitations by offering a scalable, bias-free system that better reflects real-world usage.
