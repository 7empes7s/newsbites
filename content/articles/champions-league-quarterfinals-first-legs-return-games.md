The 2025/26 UEFA Champions League quarter-finals now have shape. After the
first legs on April 7 and April 8, every tie is still alive, but some clubs are
carrying much more pressure into the return games.

Arsenal came back from Lisbon with a 1-0 win over Sporting CP. Bayern Munich
left Madrid with a 2-1 victory over Real Madrid. Atletico Madrid beat
Barcelona 2-0 away from home. Paris Saint-Germain beat Liverpool 2-0 in Paris.

## What the first legs produced

The Arsenal result is the smallest edge of the four. A 1-0 first-leg lead keeps
Sporting close enough to treat the second leg in London as a live contest
rather than a rescue mission.

Bayern's 2-1 win at the Santiago Bernabeu may be the most important result of
the round so far. The margin is still only one goal, which leaves the tie
balanced enough that one big night in Munich could still swing either way.

Atlético and Paris gave themselves more breathing room. Atlético won 2-0 at
Barcelona, and Paris beat Liverpool 2-0. Those are stronger cushions, but they
are still short of the kind of margin that makes a quarter-final feel settled.

## Which teams still have the most work to do

Real Madrid, Liverpool, and Barcelona are all now chasing from behind, but not
from the same distance.

Madrid trail by one goal and still have a matchup that feels structurally
close. Liverpool and Barcelona face the harder problem: they have to erase
two-goal deficits against opponents who already proved they can control a first
leg at this level.

Sporting also remain alive despite losing at home. A 1-0 margin is serious,
but it is still one sequence away from being reset.

## When the return games happen

UEFA's confirmed schedule puts the second legs on two nights:

- April 14, 2026: Atletico Madrid vs Barcelona
- April 14, 2026: Liverpool vs Paris Saint-Germain
- April 15, 2026: Arsenal vs Sporting CP
- April 15, 2026: Bayern Munich vs Real Madrid

All four games kick off at 21:00 CET.

## Why it matters

The quarter-finals now come down to four teams defending an advantage and four
teams chasing a comeback. That is what makes the second legs feel genuinely
live rather than procedural.

The strongest read right now is simple. Atletico and Paris hold the cleaner
advantages, while Arsenal and Bayern have only bought themselves narrow control.
That is exactly why next week's return games still matter globally: England,
Spain, Germany, France, and Portugal all still have something meaningful left
to fight for.
